# HSK 1–9 Curriculum Map — Phase 0

This is a curriculum blueprint, not generated lesson content. Counts are planning envelopes derived from the official nine-level framework, current coverage gaps, expected skill density and checkpoint cadence. Phase 1 must recalculate them after machine mapping official task/topic/language-item inventories.

## Stage architecture

- **Elementary / 初等:** HSK 1–3 — phonetics, tones, basic interaction, foundational Hanzi, short listening and controlled production.
- **Intermediate / 中等:** HSK 4–6 — extended discourse, practical/academic work contexts, inference, reports, argument and contextual translation.
- **Advanced / 高等:** HSK 7–9 — academic/public discourse, multi-source synthesis, sustained argument, interpreting and written translation, nuance/register.

## Skill matrix

### HSK 1
- **Listening:** short utterances and picture/context selection
- **Speaking:** read-aloud, shadowing, rapid response
- **Reading:** characters, signs and short messages
- **Writing:** typing and basic sentence construction
- **Translation:** micro-mediation only

### HSK 2
- **Listening:** short multi-turn dialogues
- **Speaking:** role-play and connected answers
- **Reading:** messages and short paragraphs
- **Writing:** linked sentences and notices
- **Translation:** simple meaning transfer

### HSK 3
- **Listening:** main idea and detail
- **Speaking:** retelling and picture description
- **Reading:** multi-paragraph practical texts
- **Writing:** paragraph completion and short email
- **Translation:** guided sentence/paragraph mediation

### HSK 4
- **Listening:** longer dialogues and detailed information
- **Speaking:** structured description and opinion
- **Reading:** articles, emails and inference
- **Writing:** practical correspondence and reports
- **Translation:** context-sensitive short translation

### HSK 5
- **Listening:** talks, interviews and note-taking
- **Speaking:** summary and short presentation
- **Reading:** news and expository texts
- **Writing:** formal email, report and summary
- **Translation:** Chinese–Vietnamese and Vietnamese–Chinese with register

### HSK 6
- **Listening:** dense information and speaker attitude
- **Speaking:** argument and extended retelling
- **Reading:** long-form inference and rhetoric
- **Writing:** analytical report and essay
- **Translation:** multi-meaning and terminology in context

### HSK 7
- **Listening:** lectures and multi-source note-taking
- **Speaking:** academic summary and defended opinion
- **Reading:** academic and journalistic argument
- **Writing:** structured analytical response
- **Translation:** sight and written translation with basic commentary

### HSK 8
- **Listening:** implicit stance and complex lectures
- **Speaking:** debate, synthesis and rebuttal
- **Reading:** cross-text evaluation and nuance
- **Writing:** source-based essay and editorial revision
- **Translation:** register, terminology and summary translation

### HSK 9
- **Listening:** high-density specialist discourse and implication
- **Speaking:** formal presentation, interpreting and expert discussion
- **Reading:** specialist texts, rhetoric and intertextuality
- **Writing:** research-style synthesis and sustained argument
- **Translation:** oral/written translation, editing and justification of choices

## Unit and assessment cadence

- Every unit has a mini-checkpoint and a spaced-retrieval review path.
- Every level has at least a midpoint checkpoint, end-of-level checkpoint, practice test, mastery review and final assessment.
- Assessment questions are newly authored against an official blueprint; no real paid exam is copied.
- HSK 7–9 use distinct performance rubrics: Level 7 = structured advanced comprehension/production; Level 8 = synthesis, nuance and rebuttal; Level 9 = specialist discourse, interpreting/translation and justified editorial choices.

## Topic progression

- HSK 1–2: greetings, identity, family, time, food, shopping, transport, school, weather, hobbies, basic health.
- HSK 3–4: housing, travel, work, interviews, appointments, services, social media, environment, relationships, incidents, culture.
- HSK 5–6: media, technology, economics, education, psychology, society, management, history, science, debate, correspondence and reports.
- HSK 7–9: research, public policy, philosophy, law, literature, macroeconomics, science, ethics, diplomacy, mass media, criticism, translation and academic discourse.

## Diversity and spaced-repetition contract

- No two consecutive exercises share the same format unless explicitly marked as a timed set.
- Reuse requires `reviewReason`, `reviewStage`, `firstIntroducedIn` and `previousExerciseId`.
- Duplicate checking covers exact text, normalized text, near duplicates, shared n-grams, one-token stem mutations and abnormal distractor reuse.
- A learning target reappears across recognition, listening, collocation, dialogue, reading, writing and production—not by copying the same question.
